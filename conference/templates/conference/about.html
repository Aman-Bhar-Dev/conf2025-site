ABOUT ASSAM UNIVERSITY

Assam University came into existence in 1994 after enactment of the Assam (Central) University Act 1989. Through its pursuit, Assam University is in the process of making itself an institute of excellence. Assam University main campus is situated at Dargakona, about 20 kms away from Silchar. The campus is set amid sprawling hillocks and typical landscape of north east. The campus is spread over 600 acres and provide an ideal environment for the researchers, students and the people interested in academic excellence. The other campus of the university is situated at Diphu in the district of Karbi Anglong, Assam. The university has the territorial jurisdiction over the five districts of Assam viz., Cachar, Sribhumi, Hailakandi, Dima Hasao (erstwhile North Cachar Hills) and Karbi Anglong. Assam University being a central university hosts a national characteristics of unity in diversity. Faculties, staff and students hail from all over the country. International students also find this institution an ideal centre for pursuing their academic endeavour.

ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT

The Department of Business Admininis-tration (DBA) an AICTE approved Department started functioning in July 1997. The mission of DBA is to contribute through innovative academic endeavors pertaining to teaching, research, consultancy and training to the process of developing capable managerial and human resources and thereby to the corporate, industrial, economic, social, intellectual and cultural development of India. To accomplish its mission, DBA offers through the Department of Business Administration full-time Master of Business Administration (MBA) programme, Integrated MBA (BBA+MBA), Executive MBA and also research programme in Management leading to the degree of Doctor of Philosophy (Ph. D). Admission to MBA programmes is through CAT and is open to candidates having sound previous academic background.

ABOUT ROYAL THIMPU COLLEGE, BHUTAN

Affiliated with the Royal University of Bhutan (RUB) and Khesar Gyalpo University of Medical Sciences, Royal Thimpu College (RTC) is Bhutan’s first and only A+ accredited institution. Studying at RTC offers an experience that goes beyond the classroom. With one of the best modern infrastructures and facilities in the country, RTC offers a secluded and peaceful natural environment for study. In line with the objective to contribute to educational excellence and become an institute of international repute, students are offered an international-style learning environment. RTC has been expanding its international partnerships and currently has agreements with over 50 educational institutions around the world. RTC provides opportunities for students to participate in cultural exchange programmes to Japan and semester to year-long study abroad programmes to the US and Europe.

ABOUT THE CONFERENCE

In today’s rapidly evolving world, where technological innovations, climate crises, political shifts, and economic inequalities intersect, social sciences have a critical role in decoding complexity and guiding transformative change. This international conference aims to foster cross-disciplinary dialogue on how sociology, political science, economics, cultural studies, and allied fields can collectively address pressing global challenges. By promoting inclusive, equitable, and sustainable models of development, the conference aspires to shape a world where human dignity, diversity, and justice are at the center of all policymaking and innovation.

Objectives

  • To examine how social science research can drive policy reforms and inclusive governance.
  • To explore interdisciplinary collaborations across economics, technology, ethics, and culture.
  • To assess the impact of digitalization and globalization on social justice and equity.
  • To foster knowledge-sharing platforms between scholars, practitioners, and civil society leaders.

CORE AND SUBTHEMES OF THE CONFERENCE

  1. Society, Equity, Transformation & Social Work
    • Caste, Class & Gender in the 21st Century
    • Marginalized Voices, Social Inclusion, Human Rights, Rights of Refugees, Minorities, and Indigenous Groups
    • Disability, Accessibility, and Human-Centered Design
    • Social Justice, Cultural Competency, Community Work
    • Rural Lives, Violence, Mental Health, Etc.
    • Any other related topic from Social Work in India or Bhutan
  2. Knowledge, Education & Human Development
    • Rethinking Curriculum for the Digital Age
    • Inclusive Education and Skill-Based Learning
    • Higher Education and Sustainable Development Goals
    • Research Ethics, Open Access, and Academic Equity
  3. Politics, Governance, Public Policy, and Political Science
    • Democratic Values in a Polarized World
    • Policy Innovations for Inclusive Growth
    • Geopolitics, Global Governance, and Development
    • Misinformation, Media Literacy, and Civic Engagement
    • Civil Society, Public Opinion, Democracy, etc.
    • Any other related topic from the Politics and Political Science of India or Bhutan
  4. Economy, Technology & Social Impact
    • Digital Economies and the Future of Work
    • Social Entrepreneurship and Responsible Innovation
    • Cryptocurrency, Financial Access, and Ethics
    • Smart Societies: Urbanization, Mobility, and Livelihood
    • Micro/Macro Economics, Developmental Economics, Behavioral Economics, etc.
    • Any other Indo-Bhutan Economics-related topic
  5. Culture, Identity & Globalization
    • Media Narratives, Cinema, and National Identity
    • Language, Art, Advertising, and Digital Storytelling
    • Deep Fakes, and the Challenge of Truth in the Digital Age
    • Artificial Intelligence and the Future of Journalism
    • Media Culture, Media Process, Media Technology, Social Media, etc.
    • Any other Mass Communication-related topic in relation to India/Bhutan
  6. Environment, Climate & Sustainability
    • Climate Justice and Environmental Ethics
    • Community Resilience and Adaptation Strategies
    • Sustainable Development through Local Knowledge
    • Green Politics and Youth Movements
    • Impacts of Climate Change, Carbon Negativity, Human Responsibilities and Responses, Disaster Management, etc.
    • Evidence from Bhutan and India on Climate and Climate Change
  7. Business, Commerce and Business Sustainability
    • Digital Transformation, E-commerce, Digital Marketing, Cloud Computing, and the Impact of AI and Machine Learning on Business
    • Entrepreneurship and Startup Ecosystems, Innovation Strategies, Venture Capital, and the Growth of Small Businesses
    • Globalization, Sustainable Development Goals, Corporate Social Responsibility & Social Business
    • Financial System and Markets, Green Finance and Investment, Circular Economy, Green Accounting
    • Management Practices, Change Management, Crisis Leadership, and Business Continuity
    • Human Capital Formation and the Development of a Skilled Workforce, Employee Wellbeing
    • Marketing Strategy and Tactics, Sales Management, and Retailing
  8. General Social Science and Topics Not Falling Under the Above Categories
    • Mental Health and Mental Well-being
    • History, Sociology, Development, Structure, and Functioning of Human Society
    • Legal Systems and Their Impact on Society
    • Any other topic not falling under any of the above categories
    • Note: Any other area not covered in the sub-thematic heads as mentioned above but is aligned with the main theme of the conference will also be considered for presentation.
